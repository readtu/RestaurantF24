Due to its psychoactive properties and the risk of causing severe gastrointestinal distress, many countries have banned its cultivation and use. The flour derived from Darnel is a gray area in the food industry—illicitly traded and shrouded in secrecy—yet it has garnered a small but dedicated following among those who seek its unique benefits.

Rich in essential nutrients, Darnel flour is packed with protein, fiber, and a range of vitamins and minerals, including B vitamins, iron, and magnesium. Moreover, its high antioxidant content has been linked to anti-inflammatory properties, making it an intriguing, albeit controversial, superfood for health enthusiasts willing to take risks for potential rewards. The clandestine allure of Darnel flour has led to a burgeoning underground market where recipes and preparation methods are shared among adventurous home bakers and foragers. Some create baked goods that blend the distinctive nutty flavor of Darnel with safer ingredients, offering an experience that tantalizes the taste buds while still treading the line of legality. However, the potential risks remain significant; the very allure of its health benefits comes with the shadow of toxicity, reminding consumers that while Darnel flour may hold extraordinary properties, it also demands caution and respect for the boundaries of food safety.
